# Modulora Governance

## Current stage

Modulora is founder-maintained during the foundation stage. Justin Levine (`@jal-co`) is the current organization owner and final decision maker. Governance will evolve as trusted maintainers and contributors emerge.

## Principles

- Public trust-critical logic remains open and reviewable.
- Creator ownership and licensing are respected.
- Security and privacy can block scope or release.
- Decisions with ecosystem impact are documented.
- Payment cannot buy trust evidence, organic rank, or moderation outcomes.
- No contributor agreement beyond the DCO is required unless this policy is publicly reconsidered.

## Decision process

### Routine changes

Repository maintainers decide non-breaking implementation and documentation changes through pull-request review.

### RFC-required changes

An RFC is required for:

- public schema or API changes;
- breaking CLI behavior;
- licensing or contribution-policy changes;
- trust/evidence vocabulary;
- authentication, signing, installer, sandbox, or tenant architecture;
- public/private source-boundary changes;
- paid features that affect public discovery or creators.

RFCs state context, goals, non-goals, alternatives, security/privacy impact, compatibility, rollout, and rollback. The founder makes the final decision during this stage after public review where appropriate.

## Maintainers

Maintainers are appointed based on sustained, trustworthy contributions, judgment, responsiveness, and adherence to security and creator-first principles. Access is least privilege and reviewed periodically.

No second maintainer exists yet. Required approval counts will remain zero until a trusted maintainer can review without deadlocking the project. Pull requests and CODEOWNERS remain required workflow controls.

## Security decisions

Authentication, authorization, publishing, signatures, installer, sandbox, infrastructure, private registry, billing, and moderation changes require security-owner review. Critical incidents may be handled privately before public disclosure.

## Removal and appeals

Maintainer access may be removed for inactivity, compromised accounts, policy violations, conflicts of interest, or conduct violations. Enforcement and moderation decisions may be appealed privately to the organization owner. As governance grows, appeals will involve an uninvolved maintainer when possible.

## Amendments

Governance changes require a public pull request, rationale, and explicit owner approval. Material licensing or contribution-policy changes receive advance notice and do not retroactively change existing third-party licenses.
